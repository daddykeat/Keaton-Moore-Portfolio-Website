Snack Time

Motion Graphic • After Effects • Comic / Cartoon Style

“Snack Time” is a five-second motion graphic that uses anticipation and impact to transform a single chip into an energetic explosion of snack elements.

Creative Brief

Overview: A chip begins with subtle vibration, tension builds, and the animation culminates in a comic-style “BOOM!” reveal followed by additional snack items entering the scene.

Objective: Demonstrate anticipation, timing, exaggeration, and multiple transformations in a short, visually engaging motion piece.

Target Audience: Teens and young adults, social media users, and snack or food-related audiences.

Visual Style: Comic/cartoon styling with bright yellows, reds, warm tones, bold typography, and a radial burst background.

Applications

  • Social media ads
  • Snack brand promos
  • Short-form video content
  • Motion bumpers or intros

Storyboard Beats

  • Intro: A single chip appears centered on screen.
  • Beat 1: The chip begins vibrating.
  • Beat 2: Vibration intensifies and builds anticipation.
  • Beat 3: “BOOM!” scales in with the burst background.
  • Beat 4: Pizza, chocolate, and drink elements enter.
  • Outro: Final composition settles and holds.

Assets Used

  • Chip graphic
  • Pizza illustration
  • Chocolate illustration
  • Drink element
  • “BOOM!” text
  • Radial burst background

Techniques & Effects

  • Position, scale, rotation, and opacity transforms
  • Wiggle expression for chip vibration
  • Scale-based explosion transition
  • Slide-in and pop-in transitions
  • Opacity fade-in
  • Blend mode for compositing
  • Easy Ease timing adjustments

Principles of Animation

  • Anticipation
  • Exaggeration
  • Timing
  • Staging

Process

I structured this piece around a simple cause-and-effect sequence so the motion would feel clear and satisfying in a very short runtime. I used the vibrating chip as the anticipation phase, then exaggerated the payoff with the “BOOM!” reveal, bright colors, and added snack elements. The final piece balances playful energy with readable visual staging.
